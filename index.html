Foundations

What is AI — really?

Think of it as a very well-read assistant that learned from billions of documents and knows how to respond to almost anything.

🧠
It predicts text
Generates words one at a time based on patterns it learned — not thinking like a human. No magic. Just statistics at scale.
📅
It has a knowledge cutoff
It doesn't know what happened last month unless you tell it. Always provide context about your specific situation.
⚡
Quality = your input
The output is only as good as your prompt. A vague question gets a vague answer. A specific prompt gets something usable.
🔁
No memory between chats
Each conversation starts fresh. It won't remember last week's exchange. Give it the context it needs every time.
✅
A tool — not a replacement
AI handles drafts and research. Humans make decisions. That will never change. You are still responsible for every output.
🥕
Built for your mission
Food banks across the country use AI today to write more, plan faster, and serve more neighbors with the same team.
💡

The contractor analogy: Think of AI like a highly skilled contractor. Fast, capable, professional — but needs clear instructions. Vague brief = poor output. Specific brief = something you can actually use.

The Core Skill

The RACI Prompt Formula

Every strong prompt has four parts. You don't need all four every time — but the more context you give, the better the output.

R
Role
Tell it who to be. The more specific, the better the voice.
"Act as a nonprofit grant writer with food bank experience…"
A
Action
Tell it exactly what you want — with length and format.
"Write a 200-word email" — not just "write an email."
C
Context
Give it the details it needs. Audience, org, situation.
"FBLC serves 42,000 neighbors in Larimer County, CO…"
I
Intent
Tell it the goal. What should happen after this?
"Goal: get the partner to confirm the installation date."

Beyond Prompts

What is an AI Agent?

So far you've used AI reactively — you ask, it answers. An agent flips that. You give it a goal, and it figures out the steps, executes them in sequence, and hands you the result.

Prompt (what you just learned)
You → Ask → AI answers
You write a prompt. Claude responds. You read it, decide what to do next, and ask again. You're doing the thinking between steps.
→
Agent (the next level)
You → Set a goal → AI acts
You describe what you want to accomplish. The agent breaks the task into steps, takes actions, uses tools, and delivers a completed result — with minimal back-and-forth.

How an agent works — five moves it makes on its own:

🎯
Receives goal
"Prepare a donor brief for this week's meeting"
🗺️
Plans steps
Breaks goal into tasks: find data, draft summary, format output
⚙️
Uses tools
Searches files, reads emails, queries databases, browses web
🔄
Self-corrects
Reviews its own output, adjusts, tries again if needed
✅
Delivers result
Hands you a finished product, not a draft to manage

Types of agents that already exist — and what they're doing in the real world:

📧
Email & Comms Agent
Monitors your inbox, drafts responses, flags urgent items, sends follow-ups — without you asking each time.
📊
Research Agent
Searches multiple sources, synthesizes findings, and returns a structured brief — in minutes instead of hours.
🗃️
Data Agent
Pulls numbers from your systems, runs analysis, and generates a narrative summary ready for a board report.
🏥
Clinical Support Agent
Listens to a patient appointment, synthesizes notes, and generates personalized care guidance — the exact model being built for Food is Medicine right now.
🚚
Logistics Agent
Monitors inventory levels, predicts demand, and reroutes food distribution before a shortage happens.
⚡

The practical difference: Prompts save you time on individual tasks. Agents remove entire categories of repetitive work from your week. The world moved from prompts to agents in 2025 — and the organizations that understand both will operate differently than those who don't.

Stay Safe

Risks & Rules

These aren't reasons to avoid AI — they're reasons to treat every output as a first draft that you own.

🎭
AI can be wrong
It confidently states false facts — wrong stats, invented citations, made-up names. Called "hallucination." Always verify specific numbers before sharing externally.
🔐
No sensitive data — ever
No client names, SSNs, medical records, or personnel matters. Treat it like a public forum. Anonymize everything before pasting into a prompt.
✅
Always review before sending
AI helps you produce faster — it doesn't take accountability. Every document you send externally has your name on it. Read it carefully.
🧑‍💼
AI drafts — humans decide
Use AI to draft, research, and structure. Never use AI to make decisions about people, clients, staff, or funding. That judgment stays with you.
⚖️
Read outputs critically
AI reflects patterns in training data. Content about communities may carry assumptions. Read critically — especially for bilingual and culturally sensitive materials.
📋
Disclose when required
Many funders now require disclosure of AI use in grant applications. Check funder guidelines before submitting AI-assisted narratives.
